What is Health?

A classic definition of health is that adopted by the World Health Organization (WHO) which states that health is 'a state of complete physical, mental and social well-being and not merely the absence of disease or infirmity' (WHO, 1974:1). What is useful about this definition is that it encompasses a holistic view of health. Holistic means encompassing the whole, not just the physical com- ponents.
The WHO definition is holistic in that it refers to physical, psychological, cultural, spiritual and social factors; however, current thinking on health suggests that the WHO original definition fails to capture the dynamic or action-oriented nature of being healthy and well.

Health is not a static entity; it is everchanging, depending on the environments in which it occurs. On this basis, the WHO has revised its definition to state: 'Health depends on our ability to understand and manage the interaction between human activities and the physical and biological environment' (WHO, 1992:409).
